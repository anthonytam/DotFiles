CSC 209 tutorial 09 exercises, week of 14 March 2016

This tutorial is about linked lists in C.

Please begin by running "/student/csc209/present". This records your attendance and is required for you to get the marks for the lab.

A table of contents for this tutorial appears at the right, although it is still necessary to complete this tutorial in order.

In today's tutorial we will avoid some of the string-handling concerns we've been dealing with lately, and instead work with linked lists in which the key and the data are both integers.

First, either on paper or on a computer, write a struct type declaration for one element of a linked list containing two integers and a pointer to the next item in the linked list. (If you like you could (re-)read some of the notes about structs at https://cs.utm.utoronto.ca/~ajr/209/notes/struct.html now.)

Once you have your answer, please continue to my answer and a program to traverse the linked list.
