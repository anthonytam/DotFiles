CSC 209 midterm

The CSC 209 midterm will be written at 17:10 on Friday February 26, in IB 110. It is a 50-minute test.

Be on time or lose minutes!

You have been randomly assigned to a seat number. Please check your seat assignment in my "netgrade" system in advance.

The midterm is completely open-book: bring any books, notes, etc. But please don't print piles of man pages! There will not be any memorization-oriented questions, especially since this open-book policy would make such questions completely stupid. If you forget a particular command-line option, etc, just make your intention clear and it will be ok if it would be resolved quickly with a man page reading.

However, no electronic aids or communication devices are allowed: no wristwatches running unix, no calculators (whether or not they can run unix); no cell phones to call your study partners; etc. (Note that this means that you may not use a cell phone as a time-piece!)

The midterm will potentially cover:

  • the software tools model; unix tools; basic filter concept; core commands.
  • everything we've done in shell programming
  • introductory C programming material (basic syntax, functions, extern, simple printf/scanf)
  • C data types; pointers and arrays
  • getchar/putchar, argv, strings, separate compilation
  • assignment one; tutorial four
I do mean "potentially" -- obviously a 50-minute test can't be comprehensive over this range of material.

Material relating to the unix filesystem will not be covered on the midterm. We'll save that for the final exam.
Also, man page writing is not covered on the midterm, although you do need to be able to write a usage message.

BRING YOUR STUDENT PHOTO ID, please. You will have to put it face up on your desk.

Please see an index into some sample problems regarding the CSC 209 course material. Most of these problems are too hard for midterm or exam questions, but they are good practice problems in most cases.

Also note some old midterms of mine with solutions (although those all have a bit more shell programming and a bit less C programming than we've done this term so far).

Also see the first three questions of a previous final exam.

But most of all, look at the sample problems I mentioned earlier.

If you miss the test for medical or similar reasons, I need documentation. The accommodation, in the case of acceptable documentation, will be to make the final exam grade count for your test grade too, with a suitable adjustment if applicable.
